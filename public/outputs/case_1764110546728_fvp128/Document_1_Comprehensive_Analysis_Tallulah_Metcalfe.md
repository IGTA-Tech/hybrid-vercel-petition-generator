## REGULATORY FRAMEWORK (BRIEF)

**O-1B Standard:** Must demonstrate "distinction" in the arts, meaning a high level of achievement evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition substantially above that ordinarily encountered, to the extent that the person is prominent, renowned, well-known, or leading in the field of arts.

**Criteria Required:** Must meet 3 of 6 criteria (OR receipt of major internationally recognized award such as Oscar, Grammy, Emmy, Tony)

**Criteria List:**
1. **Lead/Starring Role** - Performed/will perform services as lead or starring participant in productions or events with distinguished reputation
2. **Critical Reviews** - Achieved national or international recognition as shown through critical reviews or other published materials
3. **Critical Role for Distinguished Organizations** - Performed/will perform in lead, starring, or critical role for organizations with distinguished reputation
4. **Commercial Success** - Record of major commercial or critically acclaimed successes
5. **Significant Recognition** - Received significant recognition for achievements from organizations, critics, government agencies, or recognized experts
6. **High Remuneration** - Commanded/will command high salary or substantial remuneration compared to others in field

**Comparable Evidence Strategy:**

This approach aligns with USCIS guidance allowing comparable evidence when standard criteria don't readily apply to beneficiary's field, while maintaining the "distinction" standard required for O-1B classification.
